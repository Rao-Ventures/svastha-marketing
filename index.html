The wisdom within

Thousands of years of insight, rediscovered daily

The Upanishads — ancient dialogues between teacher and student — contain timeless teachings on consciousness, the nature of reality, and the art of living well. Svastha brings them to life for the modern seeker.

Isha Upanishad · Verse 1

ईशावास्यमिदं सर्वं यत्किञ्च जगत्यां जगत् ।
तेन त्यक्तेन भुञ्जीथा मा गृधः कस्यस्विद्धनम् ॥

"Everything animate or inanimate that is within the universe is controlled and owned by the Lord. One should therefore accept only those things necessary for himself, which are set aside as his quota, and one should not accept other things, knowing well to whom they belong."

The library

Scriptures included

Begin with seven foundational Upanishads — with complete Sanskrit text, translation, and commentary.

ईश

Isha Upanishad

18 verses · One of the shortest, most profound Upanishads, revealing the nature of the Supreme.

केन

Kena Upanishad

35 verses · 4 sections. An inquiry into the nature of Brahman through dialogue and parable.

कठ

Katha Upanishad

120 verses · 6 chapters. The dialogue between Nachiketas and Yama on death and the immortal Self.

प्रश्न

Prashna Upanishad

67 verses · 6 sections. Structured around six philosophical questions on the origin of life and consciousness.

मुण्ड

Mundaka Upanishad

64 verses · 6 sections. Exploring higher spiritual knowledge vs lower empirical knowledge.

माण्डू

Mandukya Upanishad

12 verses · The shortest Upanishad, dealing with the Om syllable and the four states of consciousness.

तैत्त

Taittiriya Upanishad

31 verses · 3 sections. Exploring education, cosmology, and the five sheaths of the human being.
